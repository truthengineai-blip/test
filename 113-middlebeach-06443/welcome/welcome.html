Structural Provenance

Your property sits on land that was once the agricultural backbone of the New Haven colony before it became a Gilded Age summer sanctuary. The "Bones" of this immediate stretch are defined by the Shingle Style and Colonial Revival architecture that rose in the late 19th century.



Beyond the architecture lies a subterranean social secret: the "hidden" boundary of the Tuxis Pond system. This area was historically significant as a navigational landmark for indigenous communities and later served as a secluded corridor for "rum-running" during the 1920s.
